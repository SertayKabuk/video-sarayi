Video Sarayi

Prep tips — before you drop files here

Insta360 X5 → Insta360 Studio

  1. Reframe in Studio. Drag to set your perspective (yaw / pitch / FOV). This is the only place you can do it — Video Sarayi encodes the flat output you export.
  2. Stabilization. Enable FlowState in Studio. It's the best stabilizer for the X5.
  3. Color profile: keep I-Log. In export settings choose I-Log (not "Standard" / "Vivid"). Video Sarayi applies the I-Log → Rec.709 LUT during encoding. Baking it in Studio too will double-apply the grade and blow out highlights.
  4. Export codec. Use the highest-quality option available — ProRes 422 HQ on Mac, or the Studio "High" H.264/H.265 export at maximum bitrate on Windows. Avoid the social-media presets.
  5. Resolution. Export at the native stitched resolution (5.7K or 4K). Video Sarayi scales down for Reels; keep it large for YouTube.

DJI Osmo Action 6 → DaVinci Resolve

  1. Do your edit here. Cuts, sync to music, trimming — everything creative goes in Resolve.
  2. Color profile: keep D-LogM. Do not apply the DJI D-LogM → Rec.709 LUT in Resolve. Video Sarayi applies it during encoding. Applying it twice destroys contrast.
  3. No color grading yet. Leave the timeline in log. Any exposure or grade adjustments should wait until after encoding, or be handled in a second Resolve pass on the final file.
  4. Export codec — depends on stabilization:
    • Not using Gyroflow: export DNxHR HQ or ProRes 422 HQ. These go straight into FFmpeg with no intermediate re-encode, so lossless-ish quality matters.
    • Using Gyroflow: export H.265 at the highest bitrate DaVinci allows (200 Mbps+). Gyroflow will re-encode your file anyway, so ProRes is wasted — a high-bitrate H.265 is just as clean after the intermediate step.
  5. Stabilization. DaVinci's built-in stabilizer works fine. Alternatively, enable Gyroflow in Video Sarayi (Stabilization section) — it uses the embedded IMU data for smoother results than optical-flow methods.
  6. Resolution. Export at native 4K. Keep the original frame rate.
